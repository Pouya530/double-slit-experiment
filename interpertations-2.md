# Quantum interpretations in 2025: the landscape has never been more alive

**No single interpretation of quantum mechanics commands a majority among physicists, and the field is fragmenting further — not converging.** A landmark 2025 *Nature* survey of over 1,100 researchers found Copenhagen at **36%**, epistemic/QBism approaches at **~17%** (up from 7% in 2016), Many-Worlds at **~15%**, and Bohmian mechanics at **7%**, with the remainder spread across dozens of alternatives or "no preference." Only **24%** of respondents were confident their chosen interpretation was correct, yet **86%** said interpretational work is valuable and **58%** believed experiments will eventually help decide. This report covers the latest state (2024–2026) of ten major interpretations and their connections to the double-slit experiment, aimed at informing content for an interactive 3D educational app.

## 1. Orchestrated Objective Reduction is no longer easily dismissed

**Orch-OR** (Penrose & Hameroff) proposes that quantum superpositions in neuronal microtubules self-collapse when they reach a gravitational threshold (τ ≈ ℏ/E_G), and each collapse event constitutes a moment of proto-conscious experience.

**What's new (2024–2026):** The experimental landscape has shifted meaningfully. Babcock et al. (2024, *J. Phys. Chem. B*) achieved the **first experimental confirmation of quantum superradiance in microtubule architectures at room temperature** — selected as Editor's Choice by *Science*. The 2023 Princeton study (Kalra et al., *ACS Central Science*) showed quantum energy migration in microtubules over 6.6 nm, with anesthetics reducing this effect — co-authored by both Hameroff and Penrose. A 2024 *eNeuro* paper demonstrated that rats injected with a microtubule-stabilising drug took significantly longer to lose consciousness under anaesthesia (Cohen's d = 1.9). Mavromatos, Mershin & Nanopoulos (Nov 2025, *European Physical Journal Plus*) modelled microtubule interiors as QED cavities supporting decoherence-resistant entanglement with times of ~10⁻⁶ s.

A startup called Valis Corporation claimed (April 2025, arXiv:2504.02914) to have demonstrated gravitationally induced wavefunction collapse on an IBM quantum chip, though this is an unreviewed corporate preprint and should be treated cautiously.

**Academic status:** Emerging minority position — no longer purely fringe but not mainstream. Publishes in peer-reviewed journals (Oxford, ACS, Springer). The strongest criticism remains Tegmark's decoherence objection (quantum states decohere in ~10⁻¹³ s), though Hagan et al.'s recalculation (10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s with shielding) and the 2024 superradiance result weaken this. The parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model was ruled out by Gran Sasso experiments (Donadi et al. 2021, *Nature Physics*), but modified versions survive. Critically, Orch-OR says **gravity causes collapse, not consciousness** — consciousness is a *consequence* of collapse events, not their cause. This is a crucial distinction for the app.

**Key active figures:** Stuart Hameroff (Arizona, emeritus), Roger Penrose (Oxford, age 94, still meeting collaborators as of July 2025), Philip Kurian (Howard University), Gregory Scholes (Princeton), Jack Tuszyński (Alberta).

**App connection:** Orch-OR rejects the "conscious observer collapses the wave" narrative. For the app, a **mass/gravity slider** is more appropriate than an observer toggle — collapse happens when enough mass is involved, regardless of whether anyone watches. A three-way toggle could contrast Copenhagen (observer-dependent), Orch-OR (gravity-dependent), and Many-Worlds (no collapse).

**A-level summary:** Orch-OR proposes that consciousness arises from quantum processes in tiny protein tubes (microtubules) inside neurons. When quantum states in these tubes become gravitationally unstable, they spontaneously collapse — and each collapse creates a fleeting moment of experience. Recent experiments have found surprising quantum effects in microtubules, but the theory remains unproven and controversial.

## 2. Hoffman's conscious agents are gaining mathematical sophistication but not empirical traction

Donald Hoffman's Conscious Agents Theory (CAT) proposes that spacetime and physical objects are not fundamental reality but a species-specific "user interface" — like icons on a desktop — generated by networks of conscious agents whose interactions are described by Markov chains.

**What's new (2024–2026):** The mathematical formalism has advanced substantially. "Fusions of Consciousness" (Hoffman, Prakash & Prentner, 2023, *Entropy*) defined how conscious agents combine and introduced a **map from Markov chains to decorated permutations** — the same structures used in the amplituhedron for computing scattering amplitudes. The most recent paper, "Traces of Consciousness" (October 2024, preprint), introduces a **trace order** on Markov kernels that produces non-Boolean logic and proposes a derivation of the Heisenberg uncertainty relation from within the framework. A new collaborator, distinguished physicist **Swapan Chattopadhyay** (UC Berkeley, Fermilab), has lent physics credibility to the project. Hoffman has also argued that the arrow of time is an artefact of projection from stationary Markov dynamics ("Spacetime Is Doomed," *Timing & Time Perception*, 2024).

**Academic status:** Speculative/emerging. Hoffman has genuine credentials (MIT PhD, Troland Award from the National Academy of Sciences, 11,744+ Google Scholar citations), and the Interface Theory of Perception received serious peer engagement (a full *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review* special issue with ten commentaries). However, CAT publishes primarily in consciousness-studies journals, not mainstream physics venues. **No experimental physicist appears to be designing experiments to test CAT specifically.** Critics charge self-refutation (if perception doesn't track truth, why trust the theory's own claims?) and heuristic sterility. The association with Deepak Chopra in popular media hasn't helped scientific credibility.

**Most notable stated testable prediction:** "No physical object has real values of dynamical physical properties when it is not observed. If we find definitive evidence otherwise, my theories would be in ruins."

**App connection:** Hoffman's framework aligns most closely with QBism — wavefunction collapse as Bayesian belief-updating by a conscious agent, not a physical event. The observer toggle would represent the agent's interface rendering different "icons" (interference pattern vs. particle pattern) depending on available information. Neither pattern reveals objective reality.

**A-level summary:** Hoffman proposes that the physical world we see — including space, time, and objects — is a simplified "user interface" evolution created to help us survive, much like desktop icons don't show what's really inside a computer. Behind this interface, reality consists of networks of "conscious agents" described by probability mathematics. While intellectually provocative and mathematically developing, the theory is highly speculative and not accepted by mainstream physics.

## 3. Von Neumann-Wigner is largely historical, but Wigner's Friend experiments are booming

The consciousness-causes-collapse interpretation proposes that the wavefunction only collapses when observed by a conscious mind. A 2025 paper in *Studies in History and Philosophy of Science* revealed that von Neumann himself was **far more cautious** than commonly portrayed — the "consciousness causes collapse" reading was largely developed by London & Bauer (1939) and Wigner (1961). Wigner himself abandoned it in the late 1970s after encountering decoherence theory.

**Academic status:** Fringe/historical. Only **single-digit percentages** of physicists endorse consciousness-based collapse in every survey conducted. Henry Stapp (age 97+) remains the most prominent proponent. The most rigorous modern treatment is **Chalmers & McQueen (2022, OUP)**, which combined IIT's Φ measure with CSL collapse dynamics and concluded: "The upshot is not that consciousness-collapse interpretations are clearly correct, but that there is a research program here worth exploring."

**However, Wigner's Friend experiments are among the most active areas in quantum foundations.** Key milestones include Bong et al. (2020, *Nature Physics*) demonstrating violation of "Local Friendliness" inequalities; Wiseman, Cavalcanti & Rieffel (2023, *Quantum*) proposing AI-based "friends" inside quantum computers; Del Santo, Manzano & Brukner (September 2025, *Phys. Rev. Research*) showing the scenario is more symmetrical than usually thought; and Walleghem et al. (September 2024) providing a refined Frauchiger-Renner paradox requiring no post-selection. **Active efforts to implement Wigner's Friend scenarios on quantum computers** are underway (2025, *Quantum* 9, 1851).

**App connection:** This interpretation directly motivates the observer toggle — consciousness literally determines whether collapse has occurred. The app should clarify this is a fringe view; most physicists attribute the change to physical measurement interactions, not awareness.

**A-level summary:** This interpretation proposes that quantum systems remain in superposition until observed by a conscious mind. While historically important for highlighting the measurement problem, it is now largely discarded by mainstream physics. Wigner himself abandoned it, and most physicists explain apparent collapse through physical interactions (decoherence) rather than consciousness.

## 4. QBism is the fastest-growing interpretation and handles Wigner's Friend without paradox

QBism (pronounced "cubism") says the wavefunction represents a particular person's beliefs about measurement outcomes — not an objective feature of reality. Collapse is simply belief-updating, like changing your betting odds after new evidence.

**What's new:** The major paper is **DeBrota, Fuchs & Schack (November 2024, *Physical Review A* 110, 052205)**, which solved a long-standing internal problem: why would a QBist assign unitary dynamics if they don't believe in agent-independent states? The answer uses van Fraassen's reflection principle — dynamics represents the agent's belief that measurement wouldn't change their current odds. This paper also shows decoherence can be understood without invoking an environment with measurement records. The 2023 Routledge volume *Phenomenology and QBism* brought QBism into serious dialogue with continental philosophy. **Christopher Fuchs** was named to Vox's "Future Perfect 50" list in 2023. John B. DeBrota (UNM) is emerging as the next-generation QBist.

**How QBism handles Wigner's Friend:** Without paradox. Each agent assigns their own quantum state. Wigner assigns a superposition because he hasn't interacted with the lab. His friend assigns a definite state because she has had the measurement experience. **There is no contradiction** because quantum states are personal judgements, not objective descriptions. Different agents having different states is expected, not paradoxical.

**How QBism differs from Copenhagen:** In Copenhagen, quantum states are objective (or intersubjective) and fixed by preparation procedures. In QBism, they are **personal** — two agents may assign different states to the same system. Copenhagen treats collapse as a physical process or pragmatic rule; QBism treats it as belief-updating. Copenhagen requires a classical/quantum divide; QBism replaces this with "experience." The formalism is **normative** (tells agents how to organise beliefs) rather than **descriptive** (describes nature).

**Academic status:** Emerging/growing. Epistemic interpretations rose from ~7% (2016) to ~17% (2025 Nature survey) — the fastest-growing category. Not mainstream consensus but increasingly respected in foundations.

**App connection:** The toggle represents the agent choosing whether to perform a which-path measurement. This changes predictions not because consciousness causes anything, but because different measurement choices lead to different probability assignments. The app could illustrate that quantum theory is a tool for *each individual* to make predictions.

**A-level summary:** QBism says the wavefunction doesn't describe what a system is doing — it represents a person's beliefs about what will happen if they measure it. When a measurement "collapses" the wave, that's simply the person updating their beliefs, not a physical event. Probabilities are personal betting odds, not objective features of reality.

## 5. Quantum immortality remains a philosophical puzzle, not physics

Quantum immortality proposes that if Many-Worlds is true, a conscious observer can never experience their own death because there always exists a branch where they survive. **Most MWI proponents reject this.**

**What's new:** The most significant paper is **O'Brien, "The Costs of Rejecting Quantum Immortality," *Synthese* 206 (October 2025)**, which maps the debate between "immortalists" (Tegmark) and "mortalists" (Carroll, Wallace, Papineau) and argues that rejecting quantum immortality creates internal tensions for Everettians. David Wallace offered a novel argument in 2025: infinitesimally low-probability survival branches would be **swamped by remnant quantum interference** from higher-probability branches, potentially limiting quantum immortality to decades beyond normal lifespan but not eternity.

On the MWI front, **Strasberg et al. (*Physical Review X*, October 2024)** provided the **first numerical demonstration from first principles** that classical-like branch structure emerges naturally from the Schrödinger equation — a significant technical advance. The 2025 Nature survey found MWI at ~15%. Sean Carroll published *Quanta and Fields* (May 2024) and remains the most prominent MWI advocate; Max Tegmark has shifted focus entirely to AI safety.

**Academic status:** Quantum immortality is fringe/speculative. Many-Worlds itself is a serious minority position (~15%). The thought experiment is debated only in philosophy of physics journals. Most physicists view it as either a reductio ad absurdum of MWI or a misapplication of probability.

**App connection:** Under MWI, the double-slit interference pattern never truly disappears — the world branches so each branch sees a definite outcome. The toggle could show: "In your branch, the particle went through slit A. In another branch, it went through slit B. The interference pattern exists across all branches combined."

**A-level summary:** Quantum immortality is a thought experiment arising from the Many-Worlds Interpretation, which says every possible quantum outcome actually occurs in separate "branches" of reality. The idea proposes that a conscious observer can never experience death because there's always a surviving branch — but most physicists, including MWI supporters like Sean Carroll, reject this conclusion, arguing it misapplies probability and that death is gradual, not a sharp quantum event.

## 6. Relational Quantum Mechanics is at a crossroads between two versions

RQM (Rovelli, 1996) holds that quantum properties exist only *relative to* the system observing them — much like velocity is relative to a reference frame. Any physical interaction can establish definite values, but only relative to the interacting partner.

**What's new:** RQM has split into two versions. **"Old RQM"** maintains radically perspectival facts with no absolute truths. **"New RQM"** (Adlam & Rovelli, 2023) introduces **Cross-Perspective Links (CPL)** — a new axiom enabling intersubjective agreement, "bidding farewell to unbridled relativism." The Stanford Encyclopedia entry was **substantively revised February 2025**. A *Foundations of Physics* special issue (2024) was dedicated to RQM. Active criticism continues: Faglia (2025, *Philosophy of Physics*) argues RQM does not resolve the measurement problem; Żukowski & Markiewicz (2024) argue CPL makes relative facts effectively hidden variables.

Rovelli's 2024–2025 publications focus primarily on quantum gravity rather than new RQM foundations — work is now mainly carried by collaborators including Emily Adlam, Andrea Di Biagio, and a productive University of Urbino group (Fano, Sanchioni, Covoni). Rovelli received the **2024 Lewis Thomas Prize** for Writing About Science.

**App connection:** RQM is **directly relevant** to the observer toggle. Different "observers" (any physical system) have different valid descriptions. The app could toggle between perspectives: "No observer" (superposition), "Detector's perspective" (definite outcome), "External observer's perspective" (system + detector still in superposition). This powerfully illustrates that facts are perspective-dependent without invoking consciousness.

**A-level summary:** RQM says quantum properties like position or spin don't exist absolutely — they only exist relative to whatever system is interacting with them, just as velocity only exists relative to a reference frame. Two different observers can give equally valid but different quantum descriptions of the same system. This dissolves the measurement problem by saying there's nothing special about measurement — it's just any physical interaction.

## 7. Objective collapse theories: the only interpretations making testable predictions

GRW, CSL, and Diósi-Penrose models add stochastic terms to the Schrödinger equation, causing spontaneous collapse that is negligible for microscopic systems but overwhelming for macroscopic ones. These are **the only interpretations that predict experimental results different from standard quantum mechanics**.

**What's new (2024–2026):** This is one of the most experimentally active areas. **Altamura et al. (February 2025, *Phys. Rev. A*)** derived the **strongest CSL bounds** from LISA Pathfinder's rotational noise data. **Figurato et al. (November 2024, *New J. Phys.*)** established an upper bound R₀ ≲ 10⁻⁴ m for the Diósi-Penrose model. **Rossi et al. (August 2025, *PRL*)** achieved quantum delocalisation of a levitated nanosphere exceeding 3× the zero-point motion — a crucial stepping stone toward macroscopic superposition tests. **Bose et al. (2025, *Phys. Rev. D*)** showed the DP model surprisingly predicts **gravitationally induced entanglement**, creating a new falsification avenue. A **MAQRO-PF (Pathfinder) space mission white paper** was published December 2025, targeting deployment before 2040 for definitive tests with nanoparticles visible to the naked eye.

The parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model is **ruled out** (Donadi et al. 2021, *Nature Physics*), but modified versions survive in a narrowing parameter window: **4 × 10⁻¹⁰ m ≲ R₀ ≲ 10⁻⁴ m**. The original GRW and Adler's enhanced CSL parameters are being squeezed but not eliminated. Carlesso & Donadi published a comprehensive review (August 2025, arXiv:2508.18822). Sabine Hossenfelder proposed a new approach using string T-duality-inspired gravitational self-energy (December 2025).

**Academic status:** Serious minority programme. Publishes in *Nature Physics*, *PRL*, *Phys. Rev. D*. Funded by EU, ESA, and with active space mission proposals. **Angelo Bassi** (Trieste) leads the TEQ project; **Matteo Carlesso** (Belfast), **Lajos Diósi** (Budapest), and **Markus Arndt** (Vienna) remain key figures.

**App connection:** Objective collapse theories remove the observer entirely. Instead of an observer toggle, the app should provide a **mass slider** — as the particle mass increases, interference gradually fades regardless of observation. For electrons, the prediction is identical to standard QM (full interference). For sufficiently massive objects (~10⁶–10¹⁰ amu), fringes should disappear spontaneously. This is a powerful teaching contrast with Copenhagen's observer-dependence.

**A-level summary:** These theories propose that quantum superpositions break down spontaneously due to a real physical process — not because someone observes them. Extra terms in the Schrödinger equation are negligible for tiny particles but become overwhelming for large objects, naturally explaining why we never see macroscopic superpositions. They are the only interpretations making predictions different from standard quantum mechanics, and experiments are actively testing them.

## 8. IIT's adversarial collaboration delivered ambiguous results, while quantum extensions advance

Integrated Information Theory (Tononi) proposes consciousness arises wherever a system integrates information irreducibly, measured by **Φ** (phi). IIT 4.0, published October 2023, is the latest version.

**What's new:** The **Cogitate Consortium adversarial collaboration** — 256 participants, six labs, fMRI/MEG/intracranial EEG — published in ***Nature* (April 2025)**. Results partly supported both IIT and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory: IIT's prediction that conscious content is decoded from posterior cortex (not prefrontal) was confirmed, but its prediction of sustained synchronisation within posterior cortex was not. Three independent judges found IIT fared slightly better overall. Christof Koch lost his famous **25-year bet** with David Chalmers that the neural correlate of consciousness would be identified by 2023, paying with a case of fine wine.

On the quantum front, **Albantakis, Prentner & Durham (2023, *Entropy*)** extended IIT 4.0 to quantum systems using density matrix formulations and released a **QIIT Python toolbox**. Chalmers & McQueen (2022) explored combining IIT's Φ with CSL collapse dynamics, proposing that superpositions of states with different Φ values might trigger collapse — simple versions were falsified by the quantum Zeno effect, but more complex "Q-shape" versions remain untested.

A **pseudoscience controversy** erupted in September 2023 when 124 scholars signed an open letter labelling IIT pseudoscience. Only **8%** of consciousness researchers surveyed fully agreed. *Nature*'s editors stated "such language has no place" in scientific discourse. Tononi et al. responded in *Nature Neuroscience* (2025).

**App connection:** IIT does not support the idea that a conscious observer changes quantum outcomes. The relevant factor is the integrated information of the entire measurement system, not whether a human watches. The app should avoid implying conscious observation changes physics under IIT.

**A-level summary:** IIT proposes consciousness arises from a system's ability to integrate information in a way that cannot be reduced to its parts, measured by Φ. The higher a system's Φ, the more conscious it is. While IIT is primarily a neuroscience theory, researchers have begun extending its mathematics into the quantum domain, and some philosophers have explored whether Φ could play a role in wavefunction collapse.

## 9. Decoherence is now experimentally validated as the mechanism for classical emergence

Decoherence explains why quantum interference vanishes at macroscopic scales: environmental entanglement causes off-diagonal density matrix elements to decay exponentially fast. **Quantum Darwinism** (Zurek) extends this by explaining how objective classical reality emerges — the environment redundantly copies "pointer state" information so all observers agree.

**What's new:** Two landmark 2025 publications mark decoherence's coming of age. **Zurek published his magnum opus**: *Decoherence and Quantum Darwinism: From Quantum Foundations to Classical Reality* (Cambridge University Press, March 2025), synthesising 40+ years of work and deriving Born's rule from envariance. **Zhu, Salice, Touil et al. (*Science Advances*, April 2025)** demonstrated Quantum Darwinism experimentally using **up to 17 superconducting qubits**, directly observing branching quantum states, mutual information saturation (classical plateau), and vanishing quantum discord. The authors declared Quantum Darwinism "now experimentally validated and established as a mature field." Kiely et al. (2026, *Phys. Rev. A*) developed a metrological framework using quantum Fisher information as a "stopwatch" for the emergence of classical objectivity.

**The measurement problem question:** Decoherence solves the preferred basis problem (why outcomes occur in specific bases) and explains the non-observability of macroscopic interference, but it does **not** solve the problem of outcomes — why we see one specific result. As Schlosshauer's influential 2004 review states, decoherence needs to be paired with an interpretation to address this. Zurek's position in his 2025 book is that Quantum Darwinism provides a "more complete" account, but this remains debated.

**Academic status:** Fully mainstream — universally accepted as a real physical phenomenon and foundational to quantum information science. Schlosshauer remains at the University of Portland; his 2007 textbook is still the standard reference (no new edition).

**App connection:** Decoherence is **excellently suited** for a graduated slider: isolated system → partial environment → full environment. As environmental coupling increases, interference fringes gradually fade — quantitatively and continuously, not as an abrupt collapse. A "Quantum Darwinism" mode could show how different environmental fragments all acquire the same classical information. This makes the quantum-to-classical transition feel physical and intuitive.

**A-level summary:** Decoherence explains why we never see quantum weirdness in everyday life: when a quantum system interacts with its surroundings, interference effects leak away extremely rapidly, making it appear classical. Quantum Darwinism goes further, showing how the environment makes redundant copies of information so different observers agree on what they see. However, decoherence alone doesn't fully explain why we see one specific outcome rather than all possibilities.

## 10. The hard problem of consciousness meets quantum mechanics in an era of AI

The intersection of consciousness and quantum mechanics has become surprisingly active in 2024–2026, driven by quantum biology advances and the parallel explosion of AI consciousness debates.

**David Chalmers** has not published major new work on quantum consciousness specifically, instead pivoting toward AI consciousness. His most substantive quantum contribution remains the Chalmers & McQueen (2022) consciousness-collapse model. At a 2025 Tufts symposium honouring Daniel Dennett, he stated current LLMs are "most likely not conscious" but gave "a significant chance" of conscious language models within 5–10 years.

**Philip Goff** (Durham University) published "How Exactly Does Panpsychism Help Explain Consciousness?" (*Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 2024), developing a new hybrid of strong and weak emergentist panpsychism. His connection to quantum mechanics remains **indirect** — panpsychism says physics describes matter's structural/relational properties but not its intrinsic nature, and consciousness could be that intrinsic nature. Goff does not argue quantum effects in neurons generate consciousness. He announced his conversion to a "heretical form of Christianity" in 2024, shifting focus partly toward fine-tuning arguments.

**New experimental frontiers** include Joachim Keppler's 2025 model linking consciousness to electromagnetic zero-point field coupling, Danko Georgiev's quantum information approach to the hard problem (2025, arXiv), and Google Quantum AI head Hartmut Neven's proposal to entangle human brains with quantum computers to test consciousness hypotheses. A startup called Nirvanic is developing brain-quantum interfaces. These remain highly speculative.

**The debate landscape in 2025:** Penrose, Hameroff, Neven, and Keppler argue *for* quantum consciousness. Tegmark, Seth, the 124 pseudoscience-letter signatories, and the vast majority of surveyed physicists argue *against* (or remain agnostic). Chalmers occupies the middle ground — taking it seriously as a research programme without endorsing it. Busemeyer & Lu (2025, *JCS*) drew an important distinction between "quantum cognition" (using quantum mathematics as models) and literal quantum processes in the brain.

## The 2022 Nobel validated foundations work but favoured no interpretation

The user's reference to the "2023 Nobel Prize" and Bell tests concerns the **2022 Nobel** awarded to Aspect, Clauser, and Zeilinger. (The 2023 Nobel went to Agostini, Krausz, and L'Huillier for attosecond physics, which has no direct interpretational implications.)

The 2022 Nobel **definitively ruled out local hidden variable theories** but does **not favour any single interpretation**. Copenhagen drops counterfactual definiteness; Many-Worlds drops single outcomes; Bohmian mechanics accepts nonlocality; QBism reinterprets measurement itself. As Nobel laureates themselves demonstrated at IYQ 2025 events: Zeilinger said "there is no quantum world... quantum states exist only in his head," while Aspect responded "I disagree." The prize's primary effect was **legitimising quantum foundations as serious science** and energising quantum technology research.

## New interpretations and educational resources in 2024–2026

**No revolutionary new interpretation has broken through**, but notable proposals include: Schmid, Yīng & Leifer's formal "Copenhagenish Interpretations" (2025, Perimeter Institute), which rigorously defines a class of interpretations satisfying four postulates; Strømme's "universal consciousness as foundational field" framework (*AIP Advances*, November 2025, Uppsala University); and Varshovi's ergodic analysis (2026, *Pramana*) arguing the Born rule would be refuted in almost all Many-Worlds branches during long experiments.

**Educational resources have exploded** thanks to IYQ 2025 (1,300+ events across 83 countries, 1.2 million participants). Key innovations include **Quantum Picturalism (QPic)** from Quantinuum/Oxford/UCL — a diagram-based method where 82% of 16–18-year-old students passed post-training exams with no advanced maths prerequisites. Interactive simulations include PhET's "Quantum Wave Interference" (still the gold standard), Perimeter Institute's four-part double-slit series, and Rice University's QonticLab simulator. PBS NOVA premiered "Decoding the Universe: Quantum" in November 2024.

## What this means for the app's observer toggle

The research reveals a crucial pedagogical insight: **the "observer" in quantum mechanics is almost never consciousness in mainstream physics.** Only the fringe Von Neumann-Wigner interpretation treats conscious observation as causally relevant. For all other interpretations, "observation" means physical interaction that extracts which-path information — a detector, scattered photons, or environmental entanglement.

The most scientifically accurate observer toggle would function as an **environment-coupling slider** (decoherence view) with an **interpretation selector** that changes the explanatory narrative:

- **Copenhagen:** "Measurement collapses the wave — pattern disappears when the detector fires"
- **Many-Worlds:** "The wave never collapses — you and the particle branch together"
- **QBism:** "Your beliefs update — neither pattern reveals objective reality"
- **RQM:** "The outcome is definite *relative to the detector* but the system remains in superposition *relative to an external observer*"
- **Objective collapse:** "For electrons, nothing changes. For massive particles, interference fades spontaneously — no observer needed"
- **Decoherence:** "Environmental interaction gradually destroys coherence — slide to see the transition"

This multi-interpretation approach would make the app uniquely educational, illustrating that **the experimental prediction is always the same** — only the metaphysical story differs. The exception is objective collapse theories, which predict measurable differences for sufficiently massive particles, making them the only interpretation with potentially observable consequences in a real (not simulated) experiment.
